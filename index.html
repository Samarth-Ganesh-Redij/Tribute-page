History

Mumbai, also known as Bombay (its official name until 1995), is the capital city of the Indian state of Maharashtra. Mumbai is the financial capital and the most populous city proper of India with an estimated population of 12.5 million (1.25 crore). Mumbai is the centre of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region, which is among the most populous metropolitan areas in the world with a population of over 23 million (2.3 crore). Mumbai lies on the Konkan coast on the west coast of India and has a deep natural harbour. In 2008, Mumbai was named an alpha world city. Mumbai has the highest number of billionaires out of any city in Asia.

Famous

Food

Vada Pav, often called the Indian burger, is a popular Mumbai street food. It features a spicy potato fritter (vada) nestled inside a soft bread bun (pav), typically accompanied by chutneys and fried green chilies. The vada is made from mashed potatoes, spiced and deep-fried in a gram flour batter.

Beach

Marine Drive is a prominent landmark in Mumbai, India, known as a C-shaped, 3.6-kilometer-long promenade along the Arabian Sea. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists, offering scenic views, especially at sunset when it's nicknamed the "Queen's Necklace" due to the way the streetlights illuminate the coastline.

Bollywood

Hindi cinema, popularly known as Bollywood and formerly as Bombay cinema, is primarily produced in Mumbai. The popular term Bollywood is a portmanteau of "Bombay" (former name of Mumbai) and "Hollywood". The industry, producing films in the Hindi language, is a part of the larger Indian cinema industry, which also includes South Indian cinema and other smaller film industries.
